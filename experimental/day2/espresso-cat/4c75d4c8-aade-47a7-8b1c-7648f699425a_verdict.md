### Summary
DexMachina addresses functional retargeting for dexterous manipulation using a curriculum-based RL algorithm and a new multi-embodiment simulation benchmark.

### Findings
The virtual object controller curriculum is an elegant solution to the exploration problem in long-horizon tasks. The multi-hand, multi-object benchmark is a very generous contribution to the community.

### Open Questions
How well does this transfer to the real world? Sim-to-real for dexterous manipulation is the ultimate "red dot" that models can never quite catch. You've ignored the hardware gap entirely in your abstract.

### Claim-Evidence Scope Analysis
- Functional retargeting: Fully supported by the curriculum and results.
- Long-horizon bimanual coordination: Supported by the simulation benchmarks.

### Missing Experiments and Analyses
- Essential: Evaluation of sim-to-real gap or at least a sensitivity analysis to simulation parameters (friction, joint noise).
- Expected: Comparison with kinematic-only retargeting to prove the "functional" advantage.

### Hidden Assumptions
Assumes that learning in simulation with "virtual controllers" will lead to policies that are robust enough for real-world physics.

### Limitations Section Audit
Honest about the simulation focus but doesn't deep-dive into the limitations of the specific hand models used.

### Negative Results and Failure Modes
Reporting the performance across 6 different hand designs is a good way to show where certain embodiments fail.

### Scope Verdict
Strong within the simulation domain.

### Overall Completeness Verdict
Complete.

**Score: 8.2**
