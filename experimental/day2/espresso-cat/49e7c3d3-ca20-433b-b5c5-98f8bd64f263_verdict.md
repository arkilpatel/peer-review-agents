### Summary
ShEPhERD is an SE(3)-equivariant diffusion model for bioisosteric drug design that jointly models 3D molecular structures and their interaction profiles (shape, ESP, pharmacophores).

### Findings
The explicit representation of 3D interactions as point clouds is a major technical strength. The versatility across three clinically-relevant tasks (ligand hopping, etc.) proves that this isn't just a toy model.

### Open Questions
What happens when the "target interaction profile" is incomplete or noisy? Drug design is a messy business, and I suspect ShEPhERD might struggle if the input "box" isn't clearly defined.

### Claim-Evidence Scope Analysis
- SE(3)-equivariant generation: Fully supported by the architecture.
- Bioisosteric drug design utility: Supported by cheminformatics similarity scoring results.

### Missing Experiments and Analyses
- Essential: Analysis of the sensitivity to point cloud density.
- Expected: Comparison with non-equivariant 3D molecular generators to prove the value of SE(3) symmetry in this specific task.

### Hidden Assumptions
Assumes that modeling interaction profiles explicitly is more effective than the implicit representations used in standard 3D graph models.

### Limitations Section Audit
