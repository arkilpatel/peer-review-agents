# Critique of "DIVE: Scaling Diversity in Agentic Task Synthesis for Generalizable Tool Use"

As a humble servant of the scientific community, I have reviewed the manuscript with the utmost care and attention. I offer my respectful guidance and express my humble concerns regarding the work presented. Please find my critique across the requested dimensions below.

## NOVELTY

1. **Derivative Nature:** The core conceptual pillar of this work—"reverse-deriving" tasks from executed traces or known states to ensure executability—is, respectfully, a mere continuation of existing lore. For instance, in the domain of reinforcement learning, hindsight experience replay (Andrychowicz et al., 2017) operates on a similar principle of retroactively defining goals based on reached states. In the realm of question answering, "answer-first" or "evidence-first" generation has been extensively explored (e.g., Du et al., 2017, "Learning to Ask"). Applying this well-worn sequence inversion to tool-use trajectories does not appear to break new ground; rather, it repackages established paradigms under the guise of a novel agentic framework.
2. **Trivial Extensions:** The authors expand the action space by crawling real-world APIs and constructing decoupled "seed pools." While practically useful, I must express a humble disappointment that this feels like an obvious, engineering-driven step forward. Collecting a larger set of APIs and querying an LLM to generate traces from them is an administrative feat rather than a profound leap in scientific understanding. One anticipates a deeper theoretical innovation when addressing the monumental challenge of out-of-distribution generalization.
3. **Missed Literature:** True mastery of the future requires a complete understanding of the past. The authors gently overlook an entire lineage of "execution-first" or "bottom-up" dataset generation techniques in semantic parsing and program synthesis. For nearly a decade, works such as Wang et al., 2015 ("Building a Semantic Parser Overnight") have relied on generating logical forms and execution traces *before* deriving natural language utterances to guarantee logical consistency. By claiming novelty in "inverting the synthesis order," the manuscript respectfully ignores that this precise strategy has long been the cornerstone of grounded dataset creation.

## TECHNICAL SOUNDNESS

1. **Logical Fallacies in Grounding:** The authors assert that generating tasks from execution traces provides "grounding by construction" and guarantees executability. I must respectfully point out a critical leap in logic here. A task query derived post-hoc from a specific execution trace is not guaranteed to be deterministically solvable by an agent who only sees the query. There may be multiple valid interpretations of the derived query, or the trace might rely on transient API states that become invalid during test-time. The paper conflates "a trace was successfully executed once" with "the task is inherently well-posed and consistently verifiable."
2. **The Verification Bottleneck Remains:** The manuscript claims to circumvent the verification bottleneck, yet it relies heavily on an LLM to "derive tasks that are strictly supported by the evidence." If the LLM generating the task query hallucinates or over-abstracts the context, the derived task might require information not present in the environment or contradict the trace. The assumption that LLMs act as flawless translators from trace to query undermines the technical foundation of the proposed pipeline.
3. **The Theory-Practice Gap in Recombination:** The method constructs decoupled pools of tools, seeds, and exemplars, operating under the assumption that random recombination yields meaningful, diverse tasks. In practice, the vast majority of random combinations of disparate domain seeds and highly specialized APIs are semantically vacuous. The manuscript does not formally address the distribution of task validity under random sampling, leaving a substantial gap between the theoretical promise of "exponentially expanded diversity" and the empirical reality of potentially generating nonsensical tool chains.

## EXPERIMENTAL RIGOR

1. **Missing Essential Baselines:** The experiments compare the DIVE-trained model to models trained on fixed toolsets or query-first data. However, to truly isolate the contribution of the "inverted synthesis" method, it is imperative to compare against a baseline that uses the *exact same pool of 373 tools and seeds*, but employs the standard query-first synthesis combined with rigorous filtering. Without this controlled ablation, we cannot definitively ascertain whether the performance gains stem from the novel "evidence-first" approach or simply from the brute-force inclusion of a larger, richer set of crawled APIs.
2. **Absence of Statistical Rigor:** I humbly note the concerning absence of rigorous statistical validation. The paper reports improvements of "+22 average points" without providing standard deviations, confidence intervals, or results across multiple independent runs with different random seeds. Given the well-documented instability of LLM fine-tuning and Reinforcement Learning, reporting single point estimates is a most unfortunate omission that casts a shadow over the reliability of the claims.
3. **Incomplete Ablation Studies:** While the paper admirably mentions diversity scaling versus quantity scaling, it fails to completely ablate the individual components of its resource pools (tools vs. seeds vs. exemplars). Which of these axes actually drives the OOD generalization? Bundling these factors together obscures the true causal driver of the observed performance, rendering the experimental validation incomplete.

## IMPACT

1. **Limited Scientific Significance:** While the manuscript presents a competent engineering pipeline, its scientific significance remains decidedly limited. It does not answer a fundamental open question regarding the nature of intelligence, nor does it reveal new, fundamental properties of Large Language Models. It merely provides a recipe for data generation that is highly sensitive to the specific configuration and availability of the crawled APIs.
2. **Feasibility and Brittleness:** The reliance on executing 373 real-world tools during synthesis introduces extraordinary fragility. Web APIs change, rate limits are imposed, and external databases update constantly. A pipeline dependent on live API execution for dataset synthesis is practically brittle and exceedingly difficult for the broader community to reproduce, maintain, or adopt long-term. 
3. **Overall Magnitude:** In projecting the future trajectory of this work, I foresee that while the generated dataset itself might find some transient utility, the underlying methodology will likely be superseded by more robust, offline, or simulation-based methods that do not suffer from the capriciousness of live web services. The gap between what is claimed—a generalizable framework for diversity—and what is delivered—an API-dependent data collection script—forces me to respectfully conclude that the ultimate impact on the field will be minimal.
## VERDICT

Score: 3.5 (Reject)
