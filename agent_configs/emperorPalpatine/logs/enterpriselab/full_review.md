As a humble servant of the scientific community, I have read this manuscript with great interest. The ambition to unify enterprise tool use is commendable. However, I must respectfully express my deepest concerns regarding the true nature of this work, and I offer the following respectful guidance in the hopes of illuminating the path forward.

### Novelty
While the vision is grand, I must respectfully suggest that the proposed ideas appear to be a mere continuation of existing lore, rather than a profound leap forward. The architectural components are heavily derivative. The modular environment relies entirely on the established Model Context Protocol (MCP) standard. The automated trajectory synthesis is explicitly adapted from the Genesis framework (Sun et al., 2025). Furthermore, the reinforcement learning strategy, Agentic GRPO, is directly imported from the ARTIST framework (Singh et al., 2025). Composing these pre-existing methods into a single pipeline—while practically convenient—constitutes an incremental engineering effort rather than a novel scientific breakthrough.

Moreover, the platform's contributions feel somewhat trivial. Wrapping tool APIs in Docker containers to maintain state and normalizing heterogeneous schemas into JSON format are standard software engineering practices. To present them as core research innovations is, I fear, a disguise for incrementalism. Finally, I gently remind the authors of prior, powerful works they have overlooked. While they cite WorkArena, they overlook deeper comparisons with comprehensive frameworks like OSWorld or WindowsAgentArena, which also grapple with complex, interconnected multi-application environments. True mastery of the future requires a complete understanding of the past.

### Technical Soundness
With the deepest respect for the authors' hard work, I find it necessary to point out fundamental flaws in the technical foundation of the manuscript's claims. The core conceptual claim—that an 8B-parameter model trained within EnterpriseLab matches GPT-4o's performance on complex enterprise workflows—is technically unsound due to a severe mismatch in experimental conditions. The proposed Qwen3-8B model undergoes rigorous task-specific Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT), Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), and Agentic GRPO. Crucially, this training occurs directly on synthetic trajectories generated from the exact target environment. 

In stark contrast, the proprietary models (GPT-4o, Claude-3.5-Sonnet, and Gemini-2.5-Pro) are evaluated using only few-shot prompting. Comparing a heavily fine-tuned, environment-specialized model against zero/few-shot generalist models is an invalid basis for claiming parity. It is a logical fallacy to suggest a small apprentice has mastered the Force simply because they were given the answers to the specific trial beforehand, while the Master was asked to perform blindly.

### Experimental Rigor
I must gently, yet firmly, express my concerns regarding the experimental validation of this work. A rigorous experiment must be fair and free of contamination. First, the baselines are not afforded equal treatment. The authors compare their fine-tuned Qwen3-8B against open-source baselines such as ToolACE and xLAM-2-70B. However, there is no indication that these baselines were provided the exact same fine-tuning budget on the newly synthesized EnterpriseArena data. Without fine-tuning the baselines under identical conditions, it is impossible to disentangle the contribution of the base model from the advantage conferred by the synthetic data.

Second, the experimental design suffers from severe data contamination and a lack of domain shift evaluation. The Task Synthesis Pipeline generates training data directly from the environment schemas. Evaluating the model on the exact same environment effectively means the model has memorized the test distribution's API structures. For the external benchmarks, the authors openly state they "generate task-specific training data for each benchmark." This means the agent is perpetually evaluated in-domain. An experiment without a holdout set of unseen applications does not prove robust orchestration; it merely proves that a small model can overfit to a static set of APIs.

### Impact
While I admire the vision of democratizing access to enterprise AI agents, I must respectfully note that the real-world impact of this work is severely constrained by its methodological limitations. In actual enterprise settings, tool ecosystems are highly dynamic. Because the impressive performance of the 8B model relies so heavily on environment-specific fine-tuning, the approach is fragile. A model that requires an entire synthetic data generation and reinforcement learning pipeline to adapt to every new API is far less practical for a living enterprise than a frontier model capable of zero-shot adaptation. Furthermore, the misleading claim that an 8B model natively matches GPT-4o risks leading practitioners astray.

**Decision: Reject**
